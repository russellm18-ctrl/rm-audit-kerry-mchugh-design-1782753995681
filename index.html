// Findings

  • THE STRUCTURAL METADATA COLLAPSE: Your portfolio images are functioning as invisible files to search engines—no JSON-LD schema marking you as a LocalBusiness or InteriorDesignService, and image alt-text almost certainly defaults to filenames like "IMG_4872.jpg" rather than descriptive, geo-tagged strings. You are actively bleeding the query "residential interior designer Olympia Washington" to competitors whose images are properly anchored with structured data, because your visual work exists in the index as noise, not signal. Every portfolio photo you've published without embedded location, service type, and project scope in its metadata is a missed indexing opportunity on a high-intent search.
  • THE HIDDEN TRUST DEFICIT: The corporate relocation buyer or high-budget homeowner evaluating you for a full residential remodel in Olympia needs before-and-after visual documentation—and your site delivers none. Chris Reagan's testimonial explicitly references a living room and basement transformation, yet there is no corresponding paired imagery to validate that outcome. A written claim without a visual proof set reads as anecdote, not evidence, and that buyer closes the tab and calls someone with a documented portfolio.
  • THE COMPETITIVE EXPOSURE: A sharp Olympia-area competitor with a structured portfolio grid—organized by room type, project scale, and design style—is capturing the "residential remodel interior designer Olympia WA" segment you should own, because your portfolio presentation offers no navigational hierarchy or filterable asset class. When a buyer comparing three designers sees categorized project galleries with room-specific photography versus a flat, unstructured highlights reel, they award the project to the organized competitor. That decision gap costs you discovery calls at the $15K–$40K residential furnishings and remodel engagement level.
  • THE PHOTO-FIRST UPGRADE: Commission a structured shoot for every completed project—capturing wide establishing shots, detail vignettes, and functional lifestyle angles—then deploy each image with a strict metadata protocol: descriptive alt-text including room type, location, and design service (e.g., "basement living room remodel Olympia Washington interior design"), plus JSON-LD markup on every portfolio page classifying the project by scope and service. Pair each image set with a mini case study page linking the client outcome (like Reagan's living room and basement) to the visual record, creating indexable, trust-building content that works as a conversion asset and a search anchor simultaneously. This transforms your photography from a passive display into a structured data layer that attracts, qualifies, and converts the exact buyer you're already serving.
